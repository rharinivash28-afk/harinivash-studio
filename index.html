Process

How I work.

No black box. No dropped handoffs. Every project follows the same six steps, from first call to final delivery.

01

Discovery

We get on a call. You walk me through your stack, your problem, and what a good outcome looks like. Nothing gets built until I fully understand what needs to be built.

02

Scoping

I come back with a clear breakdown: what gets built, how it works, what done looks like. You approve it. No surprises mid-build.

03

Build

Heads down. Production-grade workflows, systems, and sites — not prototypes. Error handling, edge cases, and retry logic covered without you asking.

04

Testing & QA

I break it before you do. Every build gets tested against real scenarios, failure cases, and edge inputs. You only see it when it's ready.

05

Handoff

A live walkthrough call, a video recording you can rewatch, and full documentation. You leave knowing exactly what you have.

06

Ongoing Support

Want someone on call for updates, fixes, and additions? I offer an optional monthly retainer after handoff. No pressure — there if you want it.

About

Hey, I'm Harinivash.

I'm the founder of Harinivash Studio. I build automation workflows, Notion systems, AI agents, and websites for businesses that are tired of tools that don't talk to each other.

Most people connect tools. I design the system underneath them — the one that handles edge cases, doesn't break on weekends, and doesn't need you to babysit it. If your team is still manually moving data between apps every week, that's not an ops problem. That's a missing system, and that's exactly what I build.

I'm not the loudest person in the room, but I'm probably the last one to give up on a problem. I don't take on everything — I work with a small number of clients at a time so I can actually focus. If we work together, you're not getting handed off to a junior or managed through a ticket system. You get me, start to finish.

I work remotely with clients across India, the US, and Europe. I care about building things right, not just building things that look like they work in a demo.
